뉴욕타임즈 기사

Businesses of all sorts are reopening and hiring back workers, many of whom were laid off more than a year ago when the pandemic struck.The rebound has been building for a few months, according to data from the New York State Labor Department, which reported on Thursday that New York City’s official unemployment rate declined slightly to 11.4 percent in April, from 11.7 percent in March.

One of the clearest signals of recovery came from restaurants, which added 15,000 jobs last month. The city’s full-service restaurants had three times as many employees last month as they had in April 2020, their lowest point. “For the restaurants, we have two very strong forces at work,” said Barbara Byrne Denham, senior economist at Oxford Economics. “Most of them are allowed to reopen, and many people are very eager to eat out.”
